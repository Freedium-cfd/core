6 Apps that Will Skyrocket Your Productivity and Creativity
Clayton Miller
~5 min read ¬∑ March 4, 2023 (Updated: March 21, 2023) ¬∑ Free: No

This month my main focus has been on writing. I streamlined my process more and eliminated the friction points I had before. These are some of the applications that have me do that.

Workflowy

This is the current solution for me when it comes to creating a personal knowledge management system that I use on a daily basis.

This has led to Aha moments when I have been using it.

It is minimalist enough in use that I focus on using it instead of over-developing and playing with all the options.

Workflowy allows you to be very flexible in the ontology that you create with it. Along with being flexible in the hierarchy, it allows different views, such as boards, cards, bullets, and to-dos.

Elephas

This is an app that sits in your menubar and allows you access to OpenAi GPT API from anywhere on your Mac. Quick grammar check, reply to an email, get a viral tweet suggestion, Outline a post, summarize a page, and so much more.

CleanShot X

This replaces the default screenshot and screen recording of MacOS.

If you want to capture a long page, it will scroll and stitch it together

an excellent app for annotating screenshots

Light video editing capabilities

Easier than Loom and other screen recorders to use.

One-click publishing of content.

It can add padding and a drop shadow to images.

Powerful Screen recording with features like recording keystrokes, combining your mic output with the output of the computer, and transposing your webcam feed on the screen recording.

You can also use it to make Gifs.

SigmaOS Browser

Webkit-based browser that can use chrome extensions

If you like keyboard shortcuts, then this is your browser. I had been using arc though sigma speeds up everything about browsing. It takes the perspective of treating your tabs like tasks.

When I first started to use it, I thought there would be a big learning curve, and the keyboard shortcuts would get in my way. Boy, was I wrong.

Standout Features include side-by-side windows, your tabs on the side, sub-tabs, command bar, workspaces, tags, and many more.

Akiflow

Great Daily Planner app

It is one of the only daily planner apps, in my opinion, that could replace a calendar app like Cron for me.

It offers a menu bar menu for upcoming events that allows you to join the video call from there.

You can create one-off and recurring scheduling links to give out to contacts.

It will send you notifications to join meetings. When creating a task, it uses NLP to parse out the crucial parts. This, combined with the command bar you can call upon when you are anywhere on your computer, is incredibly powerful.

LittleSnitch

This handy utility will block incoming or outgoing connections.

It allows you to Subscribe to lists that block known ad servers and malware. It can also be used to stop applications from phoning home.

You can see a visual map of all the connections around the world your computer is making.

IA Writer

I outline and brainstorm in Workflowy though trying to format a post and export it to medium is a laborious job. Most of the work needs to be done on the Medium drafts page. I had a mental block at first of introducing yet another writing tool. IA writer helps to make the writing process less distracting and formatting, even with images, a breeze. I write up my post, revise it then all I have to do is click publish to medium to have the draft ready. That means all the formatting is only done once instead of twice. It has dramatically improved and made the writing process more enjoyable for me.
