Creating Consistent Characters using Midjourney's new In-Painting Tool
David Comfort
~4 min read · August 23, 2023 (Updated: August 28, 2023) · Free: No

Midjourney released a new in-painting tool in the last few days. I've been experimenting with it, but have focused on using it to create consistent characters. One can take an image with a character and can change the face pretty easily.

Here is a Twitter Thread where I go through the process as well:

Here is what Midjourney says about their new tool, which they can Vary (Region):

Hey @everyone we're testing a new feature today we're calling Vary (Region). How do I use it?

It will appear underneath upscales and if you click it, a 'editor' will appear which will let you 'reroll' a specific area of the image.

If you type /settings and click 'remix mode' you will also get a text box in your editor which will allow you to change / modify the prompt for the region

Tips and Tricks

This feature works best on large regions of the image (20% to 50% of the image)

It's not a magic fix and often it may be best to do a Vary (Subtle) instead

Changing the prompt will work best if it's a change that's more matched to that image (adding a hat on top of a character) versus something that's extremely out of place (a dolphin in a forest)

In a best-case-scenario you can use the 'prompt remixing' feature as a general purpose inpainting system, however, we would warn that this can sometimes be quite frustrating as the system may fight your intent (sorry!)

On the whole, we find when you use this feature as intended (over larger areas, or with small prompt modifications) it can be quite fun! and we hope you enjoy it!

In order to use this tool, you need to enable the 'remix mode' by typing "/settings' and clicking 'Remix mode':

A lot of people have been using it to change elements in their image, such clothing and items. See Javi Lopez's post for an example: https://twitter.com/javilopen/status/1693730802670981166
